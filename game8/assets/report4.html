Design Thinking

Brainstorming Session

What would make the journey feel more alive?
Dynamic world events that change the road itself — a fire blocks the pass, a flood reroutes you through dangerous territory, a festival fills the road with both opportunity and threat. The world should feel like it doesn't wait for the player.
What creates memorable deaths?
Meaningful choices under pressure, not random bad luck. A death from a wrong call stings beautifully — it teaches. A death from bad dice feels hollow. The goal is for players to replay the fatal moment in their mind and see exactly where they went wrong.
How do you make a short session feel complete?
Give each session a narrative arc: depart from somewhere, discover something unexpected, make a decision with weight, then rest. Even a 10-minute play can feel like a chapter if the structure is there.
What's missing from the survival loop?
Upward mobility. Right now it's all attrition — resources drain, health drains, morale drains. The game needs genuine moments of bounty: a full camp, well-rested party, rare item in hand, road clear ahead. Contrast is what makes hardship meaningful.
