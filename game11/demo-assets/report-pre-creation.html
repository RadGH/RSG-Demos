01 — Concept Overview

The Storm Has Come for Stormtown

Stormtown is a top-down survival web game set in a small town being torn apart by a catastrophic tornado outbreak. The player is trapped on foot, surrounded by roving zombie hordes drawn out by the chaos, and racing against a closing window of escape. Your only hope: find your friend Sam, who has the key to a waiting helicopter — and get out before the storm kills you both.

The emotional tone is disaster thriller — tense, relentless, atmospheric. Every second outdoors is dangerous. Tornadoes rewrite the safe paths in real time. Zombies pressure you from every direction. The helicopter hums somewhere to the north. Sam is out there. Move.

The Scenario
A freak tornado outbreak has devastated Stormtown. Roads are blocked. Power is out. The dead are walking. You have minutes before the next vortex descends.
The Mission
Navigate a procedurally generated town grid. Locate Sam somewhere in the map. Retrieve the helicopter key. Reach the extraction zone. Do not die.
The Tension
Tornadoes move unpredictably and pull you toward them. Zombies close every open lane. Stamina limits your sprint. One wrong turn and you are cornered.
02 — Core Loop

Five Steps to Escape

Every run follows the same high-stakes arc. Short enough to replay in minutes, tense enough that every run feels distinct.

1
Spawn
Player drops into a procedurally generated town grid. Sam's location is hidden. Tornadoes begin spawning at the edges.
2
Navigate
Explore streets, alleyways, and building perimeters. Map awareness is key. Tornadoes shift safe corridors constantly.
3
Survive
Dodge tornado suction zones and kill zones. Outmaneuver zombie clusters. Manage sprint/stamina to avoid getting cornered.
4
Rescue Sam
Locate Sam, trigger rescue sequence, receive helicopter key. Sam becomes a follow-target — now protect them both.
5
Escape
Reach the extraction zone with Sam alive. Storm intensifies as you approach. Victory — or start again.
03 — Key Features

What Makes Stormtown Distinct

Massive tornadoes with suction physics — Multiple live tornadoes on screen at once, each with a dynamic suction radius that pulls the player off course and a lethal inner kill zone.
Zombie horde navigation — Slow-moving but numerous. Zombies spawn continuously and funnel into bottlenecks, forcing routing decisions under pressure.
Sprint and stamina system — Sprint burns a finite stamina bar. Overuse leaves the player sluggish at the worst moment. Tactical stamina management is essential.
Procedural town map — Every run generates a new grid of city blocks, alleys, and open plazas. No two escapes follow the same path.
Mobile virtual joystick — Full touch input via on-screen joystick for move direction plus a sprint button. Designed for iPhone 14 Pro at 393×852 CSS px portrait.
Full-screen progression flow — Title screen → gameplay → death/win screens, all within a single Phaser scene graph. No page reloads, no breaks in immersion.

06 — Milestone Plan

Three Milestones to Ship

Development is sequenced to deliver a playable vertical slice in Milestone 1, then layer in polish and tuning in subsequent milestones.

Milestone 1
Foundation — Playable Core
  • Phaser 3 project scaffold with Vite, ESLint, port 5211
  • Procedural town map generator (grid-based city blocks)
  • Player entity: movement, sprint/stamina, collision
  • Tornado entity: wandering AI, suction physics, kill zone
  • Zombie entity: spawn system, basic chase AI, collision
  • Sam NPC: hidden placement, follow behavior, key transfer
  • Extraction zone trigger and win/loss state machine
  • Mobile virtual joystick + sprint button (touch input)
  • Title screen, game over screen, win screen — full flow
  • All entities rendered with Phaser Graphics primitives only
Milestone 2
Polish — Atmosphere & Feel
  • Particle system: rain streaks, debris around tornado
  • Screen shake on close tornado proximity
  • Vignette darkening at storm boundary
  • Audio: ambient storm loop, zombie groans, player sprint breath
  • Sam dialogue bubbles (panic lines, rescue confirmation)
  • HUD polish: stamina bar, mini-map, storm intensity meter
  • Zombie variety: wanderers, chasers, blockers
  • Multiple tornadoes with staggered spawn timing
  • Post-processing glow on tornado cores
Milestone 3
Tuning — Balance & Retention
  • Difficulty scaling: tornado speed and count escalate over time
  • Run timer and high-score persistence via localStorage
  • Map seed displayed on death screen (share your run)
  • Accessibility pass: touch target audit, contrast check
  • Performance profiling and optimization for mid-range mobile
  • QA pass: iPhone 14 Pro Safari, Chrome desktop, Firefox
  • Final release and deployment to GitHub Pages
07 — Known Risks & Considerations

What Could Go Wrong

HIGH
Mobile performance with Phaser 3 and multiple moving entities
Phaser 3 Canvas renderer with 20+ active entities (tornadoes, zombie hordes, particles) may push frame rate below 60 FPS on older iPhones. Mitigation: entity culling, object pooling, particle count limits, and early profiling in M1 before adding particles in M2.
HIGH
Touch input precision on virtual joystick
A virtual joystick on a 393px-wide screen is inherently imprecise, especially during high-stress navigation near tornado suction zones. Mitigation: generous dead zone on the joystick center, slight aim-assist toward open lanes, and a larger joystick radius than the visual suggests.
MEDIUM
Tornado difficulty balance — suction too punishing or too trivial
Suction physics must feel threatening without being unfair. Too strong and the player feels robbed of agency; too weak and tornadoes become irrelevant obstacles. Requires iterative playtesting with tunable constants from the start. All physics values should live in a single config object.
MEDIUM
Sam follow-AI getting stuck on procedural geometry
Sam needs to navigate the same procedural map as the player but uses a simplified follow behavior. Narrow alleys and generated block arrangements may trap Sam. Mitigation: Sam uses a lightweight A* pathfind (or simplified steering with obstacle avoidance) rather than direct position follow.
MEDIUM
Procedural map guaranteeing navigability
A randomly generated city grid must always be solvable — Sam must be reachable, the extraction zone must be reachable, and no area should be fully sealed. The map generator must include connectivity validation (flood-fill from player spawn) before the scene goes live.
LOW
Safari Mobile Phaser 3 canvas compositing quirks
Safari on iOS occasionally has issues with Canvas 2D blend modes and globalAlpha on rapid redraws. All tornado glow and overlay effects should be tested specifically on iPhone Safari in M1 before investing time in M2 visual polish.
08 — Ideas & Future Features

Beyond the Horizon

Features deferred from the core scope but worth preserving for post-launch or a content update. These are not on the milestone plan — they live here so they are not forgotten.

Weather Progression
Storm intensity escalates in real time — more tornadoes, faster movement, heavier rain reducing visibility. Each run has a hard timer before the final mega-vortex wipes the entire map.
Radio Broadcasts
Ambient audio or text overlays broadcast emergency messages that hint at Sam's location or upcoming tornado paths. Adds narrative texture and a subtle guidance system for new players.
Car Mechanic
Abandoned cars scattered across the map. Find keys (different from Sam's helicopter key), hot-wire a vehicle, and drive to the extraction zone at high speed — but cars attract every zombie within two blocks.
Daily Challenge Mode
A fixed seed generated from the current date. Every player on that day faces the identical map. Leaderboard comparison via completion time. Shareable via URL hash encoding the seed and run stats.
Building Interiors
Enterable buildings that provide temporary shelter from tornado suction and block zombie line-of-sight, at the cost of slower movement and reduced situational awareness. Adds hide-and-seek tension.
Supply Drops
Tornado winds carry crates into the map: health kits, flares (stun zombies), and stamina boosters. High-value drops land in high-risk tornado paths, forcing risk/reward decisions on detours.
