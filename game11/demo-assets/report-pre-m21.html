Background
Context — What’s Changing
Stormtown v1.0 (M20) is complete. The core loop is solid: navigate a zombie-and-tornado-filled town, rescue Sam, reach the helicopter. Now the game gets a full campaign layer — five escalating levels, each a new environment, larger world, and harder challenge.

User Requests Driving This Cycle

  • Remove the "helicopter awaits but can you survive longer?" popup — escape should always end the game cleanly, no ambiguity.
  • Introduce a 5-level campaign. Level 1 is the current game, unchanged. Levels 2–5 are progressively larger maps with new visual themes, escalating enemy density, and increasing difficulty.
  • Each level must feel distinct — not just a bigger version of the same thing.
Critical path note: M21 is the scaffolding milestone. If LevelConfig.js and the variable TownMap are not rock solid by end of M21, every subsequent milestone inherits technical debt. M21 ships nothing visually exciting — that’s by design.

Milestone Details
Breakdown: M21–M25
M21
Level System Core — Scaffolding
▼
  • Fix endless mode: helicopter always ends the game. Remove the "survive longer?" popup entirely.
  • Refactor TownMap.js to accept config: { cols, rows } — map size becomes a parameter, not a constant.
  • Create LevelConfig.js: defines all 5 levels with escalating parameters (map size, tornado speed, night time, flood time, zombie counts, boss timing).
  • GameScene reads level number from scene start data and applies the correct LevelConfig entry at init.
  • WinScene updated: shows "LEVEL N COMPLETE" and a "NEXT LEVEL →" button that starts the next level.
  • Level number propagated through the full data chain (MenuScene → GameScene → WinScene → next GameScene).
  • Levels 4–5 configured with nightProgress = 1.0 from the moment create() runs.
  • No new visual content in this milestone. All changes are architectural.
M22
Level 2 — Greenfield Suburbs
▼
  • Map: 52×78 tiles (1,664×2,496 world px). Larger open blocks, park-style gaps, wider road spacing.
  • Visual theme: muted green-gray tones applied to building palette. More green texture patches between structures.
  • Tornado speed: starts at 260px/s (vs 220 in L1), escalates faster — thresholds at 45/90/135/175s.
  • Night begins at 70s (20s earlier than L1). Flooding at 80s (40s earlier than L1).
  • 5 tornadoes max (same as L1). Boss zombie spawns at 150s.
  • New radio messages specific to Greenfield: "Evacuation route CLOSED", suburb-flavored warnings.
  • Sam placed in a recognizable suburban house further from default spawn.
M23
Level 3 — Industrial District
▼
  • Map: 64×96 tiles (2,048×3,072 world px).
  • Visual theme: rust-orange and dark brown building colors, industrial palette override in TownMap generation.
  • Runway-style road markings: yellow dashes instead of white.
  • Starts with 2 tornadoes immediately. Escalates to 5 max. Night at 50s, flooding at 60s.
  • Gas canisters doubled to 6 — industrial zone hazard density justifies it narratively.
  • Sam placed inside a factory building interior (harder approach, obstacles blocking direct path).
  • Brute zombies appear from 90s (earlier than in previous levels).
M24
Level 4 — Blackout City
▼
  • Map: 72×108 tiles (2,304×3,456 world px).
  • Night starts immediately: nightProgress initialized to 1.0 in create(). No day phase exists.
  • Dark blue-black color palette — eerie, oppressive visual tone throughout.
  • 6 tornadoes max. Boss zombie at 100s. Heavy flooding begins at 45s.
  • Power line hazards doubled to 8 total.
  • Tornado inner glow enhanced at full-night (brighter core, larger radius).
  • Sam’s position marked by a flickering emergency light instead of the standard beacon.
  • "BLACKOUT" text watermark rendered in map background layer (low opacity, large, atmospheric).
  • Dynamic music intensity initializes at 0.6 instead of default — tense from second one.
M25
Level 5 — Eye of the Storm
▼
  • Map: 80×120 tiles (2,560×3,840 world px). 4× the area of Level 1.
  • 3 tornadoes from the very start. Escalates to 7 maximum.
  • Boss zombie spawns at 90s. A second boss becomes possible at 150s.
  • Night from start, flooding from 30s — no grace period.
  • Tornado sprites slightly larger and darker than previous levels (visual escalation).
  • Unique win sequence: longer helicopter escape cutscene with camera panning up and screen shake.
  • Completing Level 5 triggers "THE STORM IS GONE" final victory screen (not the standard WinScene).
  • Unlocks "Storm Master" XP achievement — stored in localStorage with date stamp.
Why Levels?
Design Rationale
🎮
Replayability Arc
A single-level game is mastered and forgotten. Five levels give players a 45–75 minute arc with clear progress markers. Completing each level is a save point in the player’s memory of the game.
🌎
Spatial Variety
Each level’s distinct visual palette and map proportions create a sense of traveling through a real city that’s being consumed by the storm. The world feels bigger than one playthrough can show.
📈
Difficulty Curve
The current single-level experience has one difficulty gradient. Levels let that gradient reset and re-steepen five times — rewarding players who improve without gating those who are still learning.
🏆
Achievement Anchor
"Storm Master" on Level 5 completion gives the game a hard endpoint to share. Players who finish can prove it. This creates natural word-of-mouth without requiring leaderboards or social features.
Technical Design
Architecture & Data Flow
The level number originates at scene start and flows forward only — no global state, no singleton mutation mid-game. LevelConfig is read-only at runtime.
MenuScene / WinScene │ │ scene.start('GameScene', { level: N }) │ ▼ GameScene.init(data) │ │ const cfg = LevelConfig.get(data.level) │ ├─── LevelConfig.get(N) → returns: │ { cols, rows, // map dimensions │ tornadoSpeed, // base px/s │ nightStartTime, // seconds │ floodStartTime, // seconds │ nightFromStart, // boolean (L4, L5) │ maxTornadoes, // cap │ bossTime, // seconds │ gasCanisters, // count │ powerLines, // count │ musicIntensityStart, // 0.0–1.0 │ theme } // palette key │ ├─── TownMap.generate(cfg.cols, cfg.rows, cfg.theme) │ → world: Phaser.GameObjects.TileMap │ → buildingRects[], roadGraph │ ├─── spawn hazards from cfg values │ └─── WinScene.init({ level: N }) │ ├─ shows "LEVEL N COMPLETE" └─ "NEXT LEVEL →" → GameScene with level: N+1 (or FinalVictoryScene if N === 5)

Key Files Affected in M21

  • src/levels/LevelConfig.js — New file. Array of 5 config objects, exported as a module with a get(n) helper.
  • src/town/TownMap.js — Refactor: replace hardcoded COLS/ROWS constants with constructor params.
  • src/scenes/GameScene.js — init() reads level from data, calls LevelConfig.get(), passes to TownMap and all hazard spawners.
  • src/scenes/WinScene.js — Show level number, render Next Level button, fire correct scene transition.
  • src/scenes/FinalVictoryScene.js — New file. Rendered only when level 5 is won.
Risk Assessment
Risks & Mitigations
Risk Level Mitigation
Level 5 map (9,600 tiles) causes physics/pathfinding performance regression on mid-range mobile High Profile after M25. Implement tile culling beyond camera + 2 screens. Cap active zombie count by level rather than spawning all at once. Consider spatial partitioning for collision if needed.
Minimap becomes unreadable at L4/L5 scale — player loses orientation on a 3,840px tall world High Scale minimap dot size proportionally to map area. Add compass direction indicator. Consider a “ping Sam” button that flashes Sam’s relative position. Raised explicitly by Alex Chen (see Feedback).
TownMap refactor breaks existing Level 1 generation (tile placement logic tied to hardcoded dimensions) Med Run M21 with Level 1 config first. All values should reproduce existing map. Write a smoke test asserting tile counts before moving to M22.
WinScene data chain breaks when passing level N through Phaser scene transitions Med Persist level number in localStorage as backup. Always read from scene data first, fall back to localStorage. Avoids data loss on scene restart edge cases.
Level 4 “night from start” breaks all systems that check elapsed time for night onset Med Add a nightFromStart boolean to LevelConfig. In create(), if true, set nightProgress = 1.0 before any timer logic runs. Audit all night-gated systems against this flag.
M21 slips and blocks M22–M25 Low Per PM Jordan Rivera: M21 is the only hard dependency. M22 content can be prepped (color palette, radio messages) while M21 finishes, if needed. M24/M25 scope can flex.
Final victory cutscene (camera pan + shake, L5) causes frame drops on iPhone Low Keep cutscene under 4s. Use Phaser tweens, not manual update loops. Cap shake amplitude. Test on iPhone 14 Pro before shipping M25.

Future Possibilities
Brainstorm Wall — What Levels Enable

10 ideas unlocked by the level system. None are committed to this cycle — captured here for future planning.

Idea 01
Level-specific achievements: "Suburbanite" (finish L2 without gas canisters), "Ironworker" (finish L3 under 3 minutes).
Idea 02
Persistent map discovery: reveal a city overview as you complete each level, like a slowly-uncovering map of the full storm zone.
Idea 03
Weather presets per level: rain overlay in L2 suburbs, industrial smog in L3, full darkness filter in L4, howling wind effects in L5.
Idea 04
Sam has different dialogue per level: casual suburban panic in L2, deadpan factory humor in L3, silent fear in L4, resignation in L5.
Idea 05
Level-specific power-up placement: adrenaline shot in factory first-aid kit (L3), police flashlight in L4 that extends vision radius.
Idea 06
New game plus: after Storm Master, replay all levels with doubled tornado speed and a 5-minute global timer.
Idea 07
Score multiplier per level: completing L5 is worth 5× base score. Encourages speedrunners to attempt higher levels first.
Idea 08
Level select unlock: beating a level permanently unlocks it for direct replay via a level select screen (no forced sequential runs).
Idea 09
Community seed sharing: after any level win, generate a URL hash encoding level + performance. Friends can challenge your exact run.
Idea 10
Ghost system: record player position per second during a run. Ghost replays from your personal best haunt the map on retry.
