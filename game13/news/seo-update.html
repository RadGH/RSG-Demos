Site SEO & Restructure

A behind-the-scenes look at the site overhaul: game moved to /play.html, new marketing homepage, and a full SEO pass with sitemap, llms.txt, canonical URLs, and structured data.

What Changed

  • Architecture: Game moved to /play.html, marketing page now at /, new /contact.html, and news at /news/*.html.
  • Canonical URLs: All pages point to https://emberveil.radgh.com/ — important because the site also deploys to a GitHub Pages subpath.
  • Social cards: Open Graph + Twitter Card metadata on every public page, plus featured images wired into each news article as the hero background.
  • Crawler infrastructure: sitemap.xml, robots.txt, and an llms.txt (per the emerging llmstxt.org spec).
  • Structured data: schema.org VideoGame JSON-LD on the homepage so search engines understand what kind of site this is.
  • News auto-loading: The homepage's "Latest News" section fetches news.json at runtime, so new articles appear automatically without editing the front page.

Key Learnings

  1. Single-page game apps need explicit routing to be crawlable. When the game is the homepage, crawlers can't distinguish what's game vs what's content. Splitting to /play.html made room for real, indexable landing content.
  2. llms.txt is worth adopting early. Cheap to write, and it's starting to appear in AI retrieval pipelines. Low downside, likely upside.
  3. Featured images dramatically improve share quality. A news article with a strong OG image gets far more click-through than a link-preview with placeholder text.
  4. Canonicals matter when you deploy to multiple hosts. Emberveil lives on both GitHub Pages and emberveil.radgh.com; without canonicals, duplicate-content penalties were a real risk.

Suggestions Going Forward

  • Wire GA4 event tracking on gameplay funnel events (class select, first combat, act complete) — pageview-only analytics undersell engagement.
  • Add image loading="lazy" to the homepage portrait grids. Currently ~60 images load up front; LCP will improve.
  • Add Lighthouse CI to the release pipeline — target Core Web Vitals green on every commit.
  • Consider JSON-LD BlogPosting schema on each news article for richer search results.
