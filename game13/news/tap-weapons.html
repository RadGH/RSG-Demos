TAP WEAPONS

Milestone 72 · single-tap combat tools

How it works

Every party carries a Tap Weapon and a Tap Utility. In battle, as long as your tap cooldown has reached zero, you can click anywhere on the combat screen — once per turn. The game looks at what you tapped closest to: land near an enemy and your weapon fires; land near an ally and your utility triggers. Weapons and utilities share a single tap budget, so every click is a real decision.

Small weapons (Blade, Bow, Fireball) run on turn cooldowns — you can tap rapidly across the round. Heavy abilities (Dragon Call, Catapult, Phoenix Feather) use round cooldowns, making you commit and let the enemy have their turn before you can tap again.

How to unlock

  • Blade — starting item, equipped by default.
  • Rejuvenate / Barrier / Enchant — for sale at the Tap Weapons vendor in any town.
  • Bow — dropped by the Act I boss in Thornwood.
  • Fireball — dropped by the Act II boss on the Ember Plateau.
  • Catapult — dropped by the Act III boss in the Shattered Core.
  • Star Caller — dropped by the Act IV boss in the Eternal Void.
  • Spirit Hammer — dropped by the Act V boss in the Primordial Nexus.
  • Dragon Call — dropped by the Dragon King in Act VI.
  • Ninja Stars / Chain Lightning / Void Lance — rotating mini-boss drops.
  • Group Heal — pass the hermit healer's INT 10 oath encounter.
  • Cleanse / Phoenix Feather — the Ancient Runestones circle (INT 13 decipher / STR 14 smash).
  • Haste / Rally — the Wind-Dancer's DEX 13 match or INT 12 talk-down.
  • Deflect / Taunt Totem — the Silent Shield's STR 13 / CON 12 ritual.

Tips & strategy

• Keep a fast utility (Rejuvenate, Enchant) equipped early — low cooldowns mean constant value across a fight.

• Catapult and Star Caller can one-shot trash mobs if you pick a dense group — always tap the middle of a cluster.

• Dragon Call's 8-round cooldown means you'll usually get it off once in a boss fight. Save it for the second phase.

• Deflect is a single-hit absorb — use it on your tank the instant a boss charges their big move.

• Phoenix Feather has the longest cooldown in the game. Don't waste it on a fight you could have finished.
