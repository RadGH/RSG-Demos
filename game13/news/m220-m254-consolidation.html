Fast-Travel & Difficulty

The namesake of the arc. Return to Town is now always visible on the map and teleports you instantly to the nearest visited town (replaces the old portal-scroll flow). Visited towns get a blue waypoint ring and click-to-teleport. A new waypoint flag on any map node opts it into the system — Thornwood's Forest Warden is the first non-town waypoint.

New Hard difficulty for players who want every fight to matter. Normal auto-heals on town entry and keeps the cleric free; Hard preserves HP and MP between fights, charges the cleric per injured/fallen hero, and disables fast-travel teleport (waypoints remain as the Hard alternative). Set on the new first screen of New Game.

Questline

Three recurring NPCs anchor a 5-act main quest:

  • Silas Veilward — grey-bearded scholar who greets you in Emberglen's square. Confirms the raids are a cover; turns out (Act 3) he's the one who let the Veil in.
  • Kaela Thorne — smug ranger bounty hunter, keeps beating you to the mark until it gets her hurt in Act 4. Becomes a reluctant ally.
  • Marek Greel — quiet hooded "scholar" who turns up conveniently in Act 2 with helpful advice. He's the Architect. Silas was his puppet for decades.

Quest nodes show a gold ! marker on the map. Bounty-board side quests start in Act 2. Dialog outcomes can now trigger conditional combat (fail a skill check → ambush).

Sprites & Appearances

The redesign pipeline has closed the loop: approved art on the review page now actually appears in-game thanks to the new sync-pixellab-to-spritecook.cjs bridge. Nine identity-locked regens went through canonical-prompt workflow (previous passes drifted because prompts were written from memory instead of pulled from art_direction JSONs — the lesson is now baked into public/docs/prompt-templates.md as a non-negotiable rule).

27 gender variants for every class are now wired through APPEARANCES. Taverns respect the chosen main-hero's appearance gender and roll opposite-gender mercenaries to avoid visual duplication. Names carry their own gender tag (male / female / either) so a female warrior no longer gets "Lorik".

Combat System Polish

  • Attack Speed runtime — daggers, rapiers, shortbows, and dragonfang daggers get one bonus attack per round. On by default.
  • Attribute Rating scaling — skill damage scales 1:1 up to attr 20, then softens to 0.6× per point. Caps late-game solo-caster one-shots.
  • Loot Chests at treasure nodes offer 2–3 picks with rarity fanfare. First-time boss kills grant a bonus high-tier chest (one-time).
  • Magic Find affix of Discovery shifts the drop-rarity odds; Act 1 settles at ~50% normal items.
  • Combat log miss tooltip shows attacker hit%, target dodge%, and the rolled %.

UI / UX

  • Difficulty choice as the first screen of New Game (two large cards).
  • Hire Custom dialog rewritten to match Create Character exactly — gold/brown colors, 480px centered, 3-row scrollable appearance grid, Estimated Stats panel, level meter.
  • Random-name dice button next to hero name on both screens.
  • Combat Debug + Map Debug moved out of the Cheat menu into Debug Settings. Panels are now transparent-backdrop draggable windows; combat auto-pauses on open so you can tune layout without the fight ticking.
  • Quest indicators, cross-zone arrow always visible, post-boss first-node auto-activate, five per-class passive trees catalogued.

Tools

New Data Catalogs page under Tools with tabs for Affixes (23 total), Encounters (36), Quests (5 main + 10 side), Passives (19 class trees), and Status Effects (58 with family labels). Auto-regenerated from src/ by node scripts/generate-data-catalogs.cjs.

New sync-pixellab-to-spritecook script bridges the review pipeline to the in-game sprite paths. audit-sprite-404s walks every character × 7 poses + enemies + companions and reports misses. redirect-assets-to-spritecook one-shot migrated 164 gallery entries to the approved redesign paths.
