What's New

New Classes — Enchanter (M394) & Priest (M395)

Two support-leaning casters joined the roster. Enchanter brings Sleep and a status-particle pipeline that finally renders crowd-control visually on the battle grid. Priest ships with a Mend overheal fix (M410) and slots cleanly into the manual-mode skill rail. Both classes have full male/female sprite variants and live in the new gallery.

Manual Combat Mode (M384 → M391)

Hardcore players asked for direct control. Manual mode adds a turn strip, a card rail of skills, a dedicated spell rail, loop-pause, mana drain visualization, and a hardcore lock. Mobile got tightened HUD strips and BG variants. The combat HUD itself was overhauled in M393: the grid lifts above frames, manual-mode buttons hold position, and 1v1 portraits now balance properly.

Battle Grid Rework (M429 → M431)

The 2.5D battlefield tiles (M392) became zone-based (M430), with sprites sized to fit adjacent columns (M431). Combined with the canvas-driven sprite path (M391), every encounter reads as a real grid layout instead of a stack of overlapping portraits.

Weapon Scaling & Build Presets (M396, M399)

Weapon scoring now uses Total Damage as its core metric (M429), which means the auto-equip AI stops trading a Mage's wand for a slightly higher-tier sword. Build presets, a hybrid skills system, a trainer that lets you respec talents, NG+ loot tables, and a prologue overhaul all landed in M399.

Damage Report Tool (M412 → M428)

Every rebalance pass is now backed by data. scripts/damage-report-snapshot.mjs simulates per-class DPS across 30 classes × 4 build modes (single/AOE × solo/group) at 6 levels each. Each snapshot is committed under public/assets/data/damage-report/, and the live Damage Report page pulls the latest. We've shipped seven snapshots in two weeks — M422 through M428.

Key Balance Examples

M428 — Border Roads goblin nerf

Border Roads was killing solo prologue runs. Goblin HP and damage were tuned down across the prologue band. Combined with the M409 hero AI fix (heal/revive skills bypass picked-skills filter), low-investment runs are survivable again without sacrificing the Act 1 difficulty curve.

M428 — Mage cut

Mage's M422 number was 7,372 — already the top of the chart — and was projecting toward 50k+ before the cut. The M428 pass trimmed scaling on key spells so Mage stays the strongest pure-DPS caster (39,812) without making the rest of the roster pointless. The class still leads, but the gap to Necromancer compressed from 2.7× to 1.3×.

M426 — Ranger nerf + role reclassification

Ranger benchmarks were inflating because pet damage was being attributed to the player. We fixed the AI sim, then nudged Ranger's solo damage down and reclassified the role. Net result: Ranger sits at 9,130 in M428 — a healthy DPS-skirmisher band, not the runaway beast it briefly became.

M427 — Companion power-tier scaling

Companions were either useless at low tier or game-breaking at high tier. The new tier-scaling formula gives companions roughly 60% of a player-character's damage budget at every tier, so a Tier-3 wolf isn't five times stronger than a Tier-1 wolf — it's about 1.7×.

M429 — Weapon scoring uses Total Damage

Auto-equip used to chase weapon-tier numbers, which meant a Mage's auto-pickup would snatch any sword over their staff. The new scorer ranks by simulated total damage in the wielder's hand, respecting class scaling. The result: mages keep magic weapons, fighters keep blades, and the inventory churn collapsed.
