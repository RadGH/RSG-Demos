Lore & World

The World of Emberveil

An ancient barrier between realms is fracturing — and what lies beyond hungers for the living world.

"The Veil was not meant to break. It was woven by hands long dead to hold back that which should never have existed. But the corruption finds cracks. It always does."

— The Seer, Thornwood Forest

The Shattering Begins

For a thousand years the Emberveil — a shimmering mystical boundary between the mortal realm and the Void — held fast. Then, without warning, rifts began appearing in the frontier lands. Goblin clans, twisted by Veil-corruption, swept through the Border Roads. Villages burned. The Seer, last of an ancient order, sent word to the capital: the barrier is unraveling from within.

The Hero Answers

You are a wanderer — mercenary, scholar, or something in between — drawn to the frontier by rumor or desperation. Fourteen paths of power lie before you. Whether you wield steel, shadow, sorcery, or song, the journey is yours to shape. Companions join your cause as you carve a path through four increasingly harrowing acts toward the source of the corruption.

The Corruption Spreads

Goblin scouts were only the beginning. Beyond the frontier sprawls the Volcanic Wastes — home to the Cinderborn cult, fanatics who welcome the Veil's decay as divine judgment. Deeper still, the Shattered Hell yawns open: a plane of fire and ruin where Hell Knights command armies of the damned. And at the very end of all things waits the Cosmic Void, a space outside space where the Veil was first forged — and first broken.

The Price of Power

Every skill, every rarity of gear, every companion you recruit comes with cost and consequence. The Veil's power is seductive — Necromancers and Warlocks walk a razor edge between wielding it and being consumed. The forge of your party — the choices you make in taverns, at crossroads, in dialog with prisoners and cultists — determines whether civilization's last ember survives or is finally snuffed out.

⚔
Auto-Combat Loop

  • Combat resolves in real-time at configurable speed (0.5s–2s per turn)
  • Each unit acts in DEX + 1d10 initiative order, re-rolled every round
  • Hit chance calculated: Attack% vs target Dodge% — misses shown with "Dodge!" float
  • Critical strikes deal 150% damage and trigger bonus effects on some skills
  • Enemies use AI priority: target lowest HP, use skills at HP thresholds
  • Victory awards XP, gold, and randomized loot drops from the zone pool

✦
Skill System

  • Each class has 4 skills unlocked at levels 1, 5, 10, and 15
  • Talent tree upgrades branch off each skill — amplify damage, add effects, reduce cooldown
  • Up to 4 active skills equipped per character at once
  • Skills target: single, front row, all enemies, or all allies
  • Cooldown system prevents skill spam — tracked as turns since last use
  • Some skills interact with status stacks (e.g. Detonate ignites existing Burn stacks)

💥
AoE Patterns

  • Single: Highest damage, precise target selection
  • Front Row: Hits all 2–3 enemies in the front line
  • All Enemies: Lower per-target damage, entire encounter group
  • All Allies: Healing / buff skills restore or empower the full party
  • Cone: Front target + adjacent units (directional AoE)
  • AoE patterns shown with visual highlights before resolution

☩
Death & Attrition

  • Units at 0 HP are removed from the field immediately
  • Defeat screen shows party state — Cleric can revive at cost post-battle
  • Permadeath optional: companions may be lost if not revived at the Tavern
  • Ambush modifier: enemies act first — no player priority round
  • Undead enemies ignore Bleed and Poison — immunities matter
  • Final Act enemies have Void resistances — bring diverse damage types

Status Effects

◆ Burn
Deals fire damage each turn. Stacks up to 5×. Pyromancer and Cinderborn enemies specialize in stacking Burn.
✧ Poison
Deals nature damage each turn. Lower per-tick damage than Burn but no stack cap — compounds indefinitely.
🩸 Bleed
Physical damage over time. Rogue's specialty — scales with the attacker's STR. Ignored by undead enemies.
϶ Stun
Target loses their next turn. Duration: 1–2 rounds. Cannot stack — a stunned target cannot be re-stunned until it expires.
❄ Freeze
Unit is immobilized and takes increased damage from all sources. Mage's ice variant of Stun with a vulnerability bonus.
϶ Curse
Warlock's signature — reduces enemy resistances by 20–40%, amplifying all subsequent damage from any source.

Town Crafting Services

🔨 Forge

Combine two items of the same type to craft a higher-quality result. The output inherits the best affix from each input — a risky but powerful gamble for late-game upgrades.

✦ Enchanter

Add or replace a single affix on any non-Legendary item. Pool is limited to affixes available in the current act. Re-rolling costs gold — escalating per attempt to prevent infinite fishing.

⚒ Blacksmith

Upgrade base item quality (Normal → Magic or Magic → Rare) using salvaged materials. Also repairs durability and unlocks hidden socket slots on select item types.

🏚
Tavern Recruiting

  • Companions are hired at the Tavern in each town — rotating roster refreshes per act
  • Each companion has a fixed class, stats, and starting equipment already equipped
  • Companions level alongside the party and can be equipped with your crafted gear
  • Some companions are story-locked behind quest completion (Rogue, Demon Hunter, Dwarven Construct)
  • Dismissing a companion returns their hire gold as a partial refund
  • Companions can die in combat — revive at the Cleric or lose them permanently (optional permadeath)

Personality Traits

Loyal
Stays at reduced wages during gold shortfalls. Bonus morale in party dialog checks.
Aggressive
+10% damage, -5% hit chance. Pushes for violent dialog resolutions — harder to unlock peaceful paths.
Scholarly
Unlocks INT skill checks in dialog. Provides lore hints for puzzle nodes.
Suspicious
May betray player choices in dialog — but gains bonus stats when acting against party wishes.
Healer's Instinct
Prioritizes healing allies over offensive actions in auto-combat — overrides default AI priority.
Greedy
Demands a 10% gold tithe per node completion but deals 20% bonus damage when fighting for treasure.
