Active Combat Layer

Tap Weapons & Utilities

Auto-resolving rounds + a party-shared tap slot that never idles. Ten weapons, ten utilities, each on its own cooldown.

Two Slots, Party-Wide

Equip one Tap Weapon and one Tap Utility from the Tap Inventory. Every party member can trigger them — tap an enemy to fire the weapon, tap an ally to channel the utility.

Turn vs Round Cooldowns

Fast items refresh per turn and can fire several times per round. Heavy hitters are gated per round — Dragon Call sits at an 8-round cooldown for act-boss moments.

Earned Not Bought

New tap items drop from treasure nodes, boss fights, and the Black Market. Early-game you have one; by Act 4 you'll cycle through a dozen.

Core Mechanic

Combat System

Auto-rounds with real decisions layered on top — skill selection, tap timing, difficulty-gated resource recovery.

Turn Order by Initiative

Every unit rolls initiative + d10 at round start. Heroes and enemies interleave — no fixed party-first. Slow status halves the roll.

Skills → Basic → Tap

On each turn a hero picks a skill (by personality heuristic), basic-attacks if no skill fits, or casts if mana allows. Tap items fire on YOUR schedule, not the AI's.

Attack Speed

Daggers, rapiers, and shortbows carry the Fast tier — one bonus attack per round. Very-Fast (reserved for passives) stacks two. Fast wielders end every round ahead on DPS.

Attribute Rating

Skill damage scales attributes 1:1 up to 20, then softens to 0.6× per point above. Caps late-game solo-caster one-shots without punishing the early game.

Armor + Magic Resist

Armor follows the DR curve armor / (armor + 100), capped at 95%. Magic resist subtracts flat from spell damage. Shields add a chance to block up to Block Power damage.

Difficulty: Normal vs Hard

Normal auto-heals on town entry; the cleric is free. Hard preserves HP/MP between fights, charges the cleric per injured member, and disables fast-travel teleport.

The Journey

Six Acts, Twelve Zones

A 5-act main quest plus an optional dragon epilogue. Each act gates the next with a boss; side-board bounties fill the gaps.

I

The Goblin Frontier

Border Roads · Thornwood Forest

Silas Veilward meets you in Emberglen's square and confirms what you already suspected: the raids aren't goblins, they're a cover. Main quest: Blood at the Border — clear the Border Roads, take down the Veil Wardens. Act boss: Warlord's Vanguard, then The Veil Wardens.

II

The Ashen Wastes

Dust Roads · Ember Plateau

Silas confides his missing daughter Lysa. A hooded scholar named Marek Greel starts turning up with suspiciously helpful advice. Reveal: Lysa's body, and a letter. Act boss: The Lava Titan, then Veil High Priest.

III

The Shattered Hell

Hell Breach · Shattered Core

You confront Silas at Dreadhearth. He admits the Ashen Veil compromised him decades ago. Player choice: spare him or end it. Act boss: Archfiend Malgrath, then The Emberveil Sovereign.

IV

The Cosmic Void

Cosmic Rift · Eternal Void

Kaela Thorne — the smug ranger who kept beating you to bounties — shows up an ally this time. She's been tracking the cult through the portal. Act boss: The Void Herald, then The Unraveler.

V

The Primordial Abyss

Abyssal Depths · Primordial Nexus

The shards point at a scholar. Kaela recognises Marek. Climax: The Architect — Marek — in full. Silas was his puppet for decades. Act boss: The Primordial King, then The Architect.

VI

The Dragon's Reach optional epilogue

Dragon's Reach · Dragon Throne

With the Veil sealed, the Dragon-kin stir. A separate arc with its own bosses, its own gear (dragonscale, dragonfang), and four dragon companions to recruit. Final boss: Bahamorth, the Dragon King.
